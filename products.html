Mithila Paag

INR 1601/- INR 1001/-

The Mithila Paag is a traditional headgear from the Mithila region of Bihar, India, and parts of Nepal, serving as a powerful symbol of honor, respect, and cultural identity. Historically dating back to prehistoric times when it was made of plant leaves, the modern Paag is typically crafted from cotton, silk, or satin. Its colors carry deep ceremonial significance: red is worn by grooms and during sacred rituals, mustard (yellow) is for wedding guests, and white is traditionally donned by elders. Recognizing its heritage, the Indian government issued a commemorative postage stamp featuring the Paag in 2017.
